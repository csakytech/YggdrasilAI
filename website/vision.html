Our Dream

Talk to your computer — and watch it do the work.

Imagine sitting down at your computer and simply saying what you want done — and it happens. You open and run programs, write and edit documents, manage your files, pull up live information, and plan your day, all by talking, in plain language. No menus to hunt through, no syntax to memorize. You speak; it acts.

Now imagine going further. You say: "Let's build an accounting program." ThorOS spins up a team of specialist agents — one strong at math, one at Python, one at economics — sets up a development environment, opens Visual Studio Code (or installs it if you don't have it yet), asks you the questions that matter, drafts the project plan, a working document, and a schedule, and starts building it with you — you directing, by voice. That is the dream: not a chatbot that talks, but an operating system that does.

It's a huge undertaking and it will take time to fully realize — but that is the aim of ThorOS, and we're building toward it in the open, one capability at a time.

A computer for everyone — including people locked out of one today

Almost all software assumes you can use a keyboard and a mouse. Millions of people can't — severe arthritis, injury, tremor, paralysis, no use of their hands at all — and are quietly shut out of the web, of work, of creating anything on a computer.

A computer you operate entirely by voice changes that. With nothing but speech, someone who cannot type can browse, write, communicate, manage their day, and build real things — in real time, independently. For most people ThorOS is a faster, friendlier way to work. For these users, it's a way back in. We're building it for them from the ground up, with their input.

Your AI, your way

You decide where your intelligence runs. Keep it entirely on your own machine — private, offline-capable, answering to you alone. Or connect a cloud model when you want a larger knowledge base, faster responses, or you're on lighter hardware that can't run a big model locally. Most assistants force you into someone else's data center; ThorOS makes local the default and the cloud your choice. The point isn't to be anti-cloud — it's that the decision belongs to you, not a corporation.

Where we're headed

Today ThorOS is a working preview — a voice-first desktop with agents that genuinely operate the machine, do live research, and run on a schedule. Tomorrow it's a platform: you ask for an outcome, and a team of agents — each a specialist — plans it, sets up the tools, and builds it, while you stay in the director's chair. Anyone will be able to create an agent that masters a program and share it, so the system grows new abilities the way you'd download an idea.

A computer that doesn't just answer you — it works alongside you. It's early, it's open, and it's built in the open. If this resonates with you, come build it with us.
