Goal-Oriented Computing

You state the outcome you want. The computer handles the logistics.

For fifty years, computers have forced people to think like machines. Want to write a book? Then learn the machine's way: open the file manager, create a folder, find the word processor, make a document, name it, set the margins — then you may begin. Every step is about the computer's logistics, not your goal. Entire generations decided computers "weren't for them" — not because they couldn't think, but because they didn't care to think like a machine.

ThorOS flips it. You say: "I'd like to write a book — can you set things up so I can start?" The folders appear, the writing space opens, and you're looking at a blank page, ready to write. You stated the goal; the operating system did the logistics. That's the whole idea, and it changes who a computer is for.

One computer, every kind of user

The same OS meets you wherever you are — across generations, abilities, and ambitions.

⚡

If you live on your computer

Run it like a mission control. Specialist AI agents work for you around the clock — researching, scheduling, writing, running commands, watching your briefings — each job on the model best suited to it, local or cloud, side by side. You direct; they execute. Everything a team could do, at the speed of speech.

🎙️

If you can't use a keyboard or mouse

Then this computer was built for you first. Everything the power user does — browsing, writing, files, apps, whole projects — works entirely by voice. Not a bolted-on accessibility mode: voice is the interface, for everyone. Injury, tremor, paralysis, arthritis — none of it should lock anyone out of the digital world again.

🌱

If computers never felt made for you

No menus to memorize, no "right way" to do things, nothing to break. Just ask, the way you'd ask a person: "Can you help me write a letter?" "Can you find my photos?" "What was I working on yesterday?" If it needs setting up, it sets it up. You bring the goal — decades of computer experience not required.

The dream we're building toward

Now imagine going further. You say: "Let's build an accounting program." ThorOS spins up a team of specialist agents — one strong at planning, one at Python, one at economics — sets up a development environment, opens the editor (or installs it if you don't have it yet), asks you the questions that matter, drafts the project plan, and starts building it with you — you directing, by voice. Not a chatbot that talks about work: an operating system that does the work.

It's a huge undertaking and it will take time to fully realize — but that is the aim of ThorOS, and we're building toward it in the open, one capability at a time.

Your AI, your way

You decide where your intelligence runs. Keep it entirely on your own machine — private, offline-capable, answering to you alone. Or connect a cloud model when you want a larger knowledge base, faster responses, or you're on lighter hardware that can't run a big model locally. Most assistants force you into someone else's data center; ThorOS makes local the default and the cloud your choice. The point isn't to be anti-cloud — it's that the decision belongs to you, not a corporation.

Where we're headed

Today ThorOS is a working preview — a voice-first desktop with agents that genuinely operate the machine, do live research, run on a schedule, and update themselves in place. Tomorrow it's a platform: you ask for an outcome, and a team of agents — each a specialist, each on the right model for its job — plans it, sets up the tools, and builds it, while you stay in the director's chair. Anyone will be able to create an agent that masters a program and share it, so the system grows new abilities the way you'd download an idea.

A computer that doesn't just answer you — it works alongside you. Whether you've used computers for forty years or avoided them for forty years. It's early, it's open, and it's built in the open. If this resonates with you, come build it with us.
