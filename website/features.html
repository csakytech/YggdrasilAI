Features

ThorOS is an assistant that actually operates your computer — locally, safely, and out loud. Here's what it can do today.

🎙️

A local-first voice assistant

Say the wake word, then your request. Wake-word detection, speech-to-text (Whisper), the language model (via Ollama), and the spoken voice (Piper) run on your own machine by default — nothing leaves unless you choose to connect a cloud model for more power. Prefer to type? A chat window gives you the same assistant.

🧠

It plans, then a team of agents acts

Your words go to an on-device planner that decides what to do and routes it to specialized agents: files, applications, documents, the terminal, live research, reminders, memory, and security. Each does one job well — and it's doing real work, not just chatting.

🙌

It never dead-ends

If no skill fits your request, a reasoning backbone still helps to the maximum: it answers the question, tells you the simple way to trigger the right skill, or honestly offers the closest thing it can do — never a flat "I can't, try again."

🌐

Live answers, reminders & briefings

"What's the price of Bitcoin?" "What's the weather?" — it looks things up on the web and reads back a short answer. Ask it to remember: "remind me an hour before my meeting," or "give me the Bitcoin report every weekday at 9 am," and it speaks up at the right time.

🗂️

It understands real, messy file names — and confirms before deleting

You don't have to pronounce a filename perfectly. Say "the reports folder," "my vacation photos," or "the first one," and it matches what's actually on disk. And anything destructive — delete, rename, move — always reads back the resolved name and waits for your yes or no first.

🎯

Context-aware — it knows what you're looking at

Open a terminal and say "list files" and it runs ls right there. Focus a document and "make it bold" formats it. The same words do the right thing depending on the window in front of you.

📄

Deep control of real applications

Beyond clicking menus, ThorOS speaks LibreOffice's own command language: "replace michael with Michael," "insert a page break," "export this to PDF" — driven through the application's real API, not fragile screen automation.

⌨️

Turn a request into a command — run safely (preview)

There's no voice app for everything — but Linux already has a command for almost anything. Ask "use the terminal to convert this to a PDF," and ThorOS works out the command, shows you exactly what it'll do, and runs it inside a sandbox locked to a single folder, so a wrong command can't escape.

🧩

A community marketplace — install capabilities, not software

Browse a catalog and install AI agents by voice — hear what an agent can do, confirm, and it's usable instantly. Untrusted community agents run inside a sandbox (read-only system, their own folder only, no network); reviewed agents run trusted. Anyone can build and publish one.

💬

It explains itself — and remembers you

Ask "why did you do that?" and it tells you the genuine reasoning behind its last action. Tell it your name, your preferences, the way you work, and it remembers across sessions — in a plain file on your disk that you own and can read.

🛡️

You're in control

The AI never touches the system directly. Routine actions just happen; anything risky — deleting files, running commands — is gated behind a confirmation or an authorization code, with a hard block that refuses catastrophic commands like wiping a disk.

Built for people who can't use their hands

ThorOS is voice-first by design — so people with severe arthritis, injuries, or no hand use can operate a computer and the web independently, in real time. Most software is built for a keyboard and mouse. This isn't. That's the whole point.

🌍

Works offline. Scales up when you want.

Everything runs on-device by design — private, and yours. The same codebase also supports an optional cloud brain or a thin-client mode for lighter hardware: opt-in, never required, never a fork. Your AI, your choice.
